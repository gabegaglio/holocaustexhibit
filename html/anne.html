Anne Frank (1929-1945)

Early Life Annelies Marie Frank (1929-1945), also known as Anne Franke was a 16 year old Jewish girl born in Germany, Frankfurt am Main. She lived under her parents Otto and Edith and older sister Margot. In 1934, she moved to the city of Amsterdam, Netherlands due to the anti-semitism present in Germany. In May 1940 the Nazi's invaded the Netherlands, forcing Anne to a seperate Jewish school. A year later in 1941 the Star of David was implemented by the Nazis to be worn.

Hiding In July 1942 Margot was faced with deportation, forcing the family into hiding in the 'Secret Annex'. On her 13th birthday, June 1942, just a month before they went into hiding, she recieved a diary which contained her documented first hand accounts of her experiences and time in hiding for 2 years (1942-1944). The diary contained all that came across her mind and serves as a insight to what those forced into hiding felt on a daily basis.

Deportation Unfortunately, in August 1944 the annex was discovered and her entire family was taken to Auschwitz-Birkenau. In November 1944, Anne and her sister were taken to the Bergen-Belson concentration camp where they both contracted Typhus and died in February 1945.
