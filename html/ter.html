The living conditions in Theresienstadt were intentionally designed to expedite the death of its inmates, and was also used for propaganda purposes to decieve the Red Cross into thinking these concentration camps were safe. Unlike other ghettos, forced labor was not a significant economic factor.

Demographic The ghetto was initially populated with Czech Jews in November of 1941, followed by German, Austrian, Dutch, and Danish Jews in subsequent years. Towards the end of the war, prisoners from various nationalities were also sent to Theresienstadt.

Casualties Approximately 33,000 people died in the ghetto due to malnutrition and disease. Over 88,000 individuals were held there for extended periods before being deported to extermination camps and other killing sites. The involvement of the Jewish Council in selecting individuals for deportation has been a subject of controversy. The total number of survivors was around 23,000, including 4,000 deportees who managed to survive.
